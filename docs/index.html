Vecrypto

How to play

Vecrypto is a game about decoding and intercepting the message sent between two AI players. It is a single player variant of the greatest board game of all time Decrypto.

Alice and Bob are robots trying to communicate hidden information.
Alice wants to send a message to Bob containing a sequence of three numbers without it being intercepted.
They both have the same four secret words that they are using as a key.
Alice will generate a message of three new words that each allude to one of the secret words.
Bob can then decode the sequence by looking at each word in the message and comparing it to the secret words.

It's your job to intercept the message and try to decode it before Bob can.
You can see the history of sent messages and the decoded sequence.
You should use this to try and guess at the hidden words to crack two sent messages to win.

Explanation Vecrypto works by using word vector embeddings.

Embeddings map words to a position in a vector space where "similar" words are mapped "close" positions in the space.
For example "desk" and "chair" may be mapped to close vectors but "desk" and "campfire" would be mapped to very far positions.
Using such an embedding the AI player can find words that are similar to the hidden words to set as clues.

Currently clues are built from GloVe embeddings.
